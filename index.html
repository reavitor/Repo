Mixed Market Economy

  1. protects the rights of public property and capitalism, but also allows for governments to interfere in economic activities in order to achieve social aims
  2. mixed economies are less efficient than pure free markets
  3. Government places limits on the nature of business activity: restricting monopoly, control pollution from factories.
  4. currently Iceland, Sweden, France, the United Kingdom, the United States, Russia and China are mixed market economies.
